Roughly one year ago I handed over a software project I bootstrapped. Time-window was pretty short and resource required economical.

In the same company a few years before I was required to fix a pressing issue that could lead to severe financial liability. Business-rules were lost, system was blocked.

Even though the scale and timeframe differed. What made both these critical topics click was not the technology, but the team dynamics.

follow up

Documentation.

  • one jargon-doc to map concepts
  • one periodic-meetings doc
  • an open-point/arbitrage doc
  • a communication channel for people, another one for bots
  • one master-doc which links to all the other docs

Typical ceremony.

  • no fixed ceremony
  • typically need some frequent (daily may be too strict, weekly to slow, adapt depending on the pace and urgency)

Team and technologies.

  • people will naturally specialize on sub-parts of the project
  • it’s okay to live with low bus factor
  • set boundaries: key design invariants, and vague goals
  • be intransigent on the boundaries
  • do with what the team knows
  • set the tempo, emulation and resting are both important
  • avoid forcing new patterns/tools on people who are here to help
  • better (e.g., setting boundaries) to compensate for the flaws you foresee
  • request external help for background improvement (e.g., running in the new kube cluster rather than on VMs)

Bonus.

  • if you have a third-party external judge (e.g., auditors for financial topics, lawyers for specific regulations) they are good as they reduce decision paralysis around ‘good-enough’
